I am Alexander Schperberg, a Research Scientist in the Optimization and Robotics (OR) group at [Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories](https://www.merl.com). I am working on Human-Robot Collaboration within the loco-manipulation domain. Concurrently, I am also working on Contextual Active SLAM, to enable robots to autonomously navigate environments while accounting for semantic cues and user-directives through vision language and large language models. Overall, I have strong hands-on expertise to ensure my research can be applied to real robotic systems, and have experience across a wide variety of robotic platforms which include: Bipeds (MOBIUS, Agibot), Quadrupeds (A1 Unitree, Go2 Unitree, ALPHRED), Manipulator Arms (MELFA, AgileX) Turtlebot, and Drones (CrazyFly).

I hold a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), advised by Professor Dennis Hong from the Robotics and Mechanisms Laboratories [RoMeLa](https://www.romela.org). My researched primarily focused on unifying model and learning-based techniques for planning, control, and estimation of robotic systems. My works during my Ph.D span from heterogeneous Active SLAM for multi-agent systems, force control for climbing loco-manipulation, state estimation of legged robots, to auto-tuning planners and control parameters for robotic systems.
